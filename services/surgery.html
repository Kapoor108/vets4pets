🔧 Types of Surgeries

🔵 Routine Surgeries

  • Spaying – Female sterilisation. Prevents uterine infections, cancer, and unwanted pregnancies.
  • Neutering – Male sterilisation. Reduces aggression, roaming, and testicular cancer risk.

🟡 Soft Tissue Surgeries

  • Tumor Removal – Excision of benign or malignant masses with histopathology.
  • Wound Repair – Laceration closure, skin grafts, and reconstructive procedures.
  • Abscess Treatment – Drainage, debridement, and closure of infected tissue.

🔴 Emergency Surgeries

  • Foreign Body Removal – Extraction of swallowed objects from stomach or intestines.
  • C-Section – Emergency delivery when natural birth is not possible.
  • Trauma Surgery – Fracture repair, internal bleeding control after accidents.

🛠️ Pre-Surgery Protocol

Physical Examination

Complete health assessment to confirm your pet is fit for anaesthesia and surgery.

🩸

Pre-Surgical Blood Tests

CBC and organ function tests to detect any hidden conditions that could affect surgery safety.

🍽️

Fasting Instructions

Pets must fast 8–12 hours before surgery to prevent anaesthesia complications.

💤 During Surgery

01

General Anaesthesia

Safe, calibrated anaesthesia administered by our trained team for a pain-free procedure.

02

Continuous Monitoring

Heart rate, oxygen saturation, temperature and blood pressure monitored throughout.

03

Sterile Environment

Full surgical sterilisation protocols to prevent post-operative infections.

Post-Surgery Care

Pain management medications prescribed
Antibiotics to prevent infection
Detailed wound care instructions given
E-collar (cone) provided if needed
Scheduled follow-up visits
24-hour contact for post-op concerns
