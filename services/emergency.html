⚡ Common Pet Emergencies

🚗

Road Accidents

Trauma, fractures, internal bleeding from vehicle collisions.

☠️

Poisoning

Ingestion of toxic foods, plants, chemicals or medications.

🤢

Severe Vomiting / Diarrhoea

Persistent episodes causing dehydration and weakness.

😮‍💨

Breathing Difficulty

Laboured breathing, choking, or respiratory distress.

⚡

Seizures

Uncontrolled convulsions requiring immediate stabilisation.

🌡️

Heatstroke

Overheating causing collapse, panting, and organ failure.

🩸

Severe Bleeding

Deep wounds, lacerations or internal haemorrhage.

😵

Collapse / Unconsciousness

Sudden loss of consciousness or inability to stand.

What To Do in an Emergency

1

Stay Calm

Your pet senses your anxiety. Stay as calm as possible to avoid stressing them further.

2

Call Us First

Call 06366965129 before arriving so we can prepare for your pet's arrival.

3

Don't Delay

Time is critical in emergencies. Do not wait to see if the pet improves on its own.

4

Bring All Information

Bring any medications, toxins ingested, or medical history you have available.

5

Handle Carefully

Injured animals may bite from pain. Use a towel or blanket to safely transport them.

☠️ Common Toxins — Keep Away from Pets

🐕Dogs
  • Chocolate & caffeine
  • Grapes & raisins
  • Onions & garlic
  • Xylitol (sweetener)
  • Rat poison
  • Human medications
🐈Cats
  • Lilies (all parts)
  • Essential oils
  • Paracetamol / Aspirin
  • Onions & garlic
  • Dog flea products
  • Antifreeze
