😬 Common Dental Problems in Pets

Tartar Buildup

Hardened plaque on teeth that causes inflammation, pain and eventual tooth loss if untreated.

💨

Bad Breath (Halitosis)

Often the first sign of dental disease. Caused by bacteria accumulating in the mouth.

🔴

Gingivitis

Gum inflammation caused by bacterial infection. Leads to bleeding, pain and difficulty eating.

❌

Tooth Decay & Loss

Advanced dental disease can cause teeth to rot and fall out, affecting eating and quality of life.

⚠️ Warning Signs to Watch For

⚠️ Bad smell from mouth
⚠️ Difficulty eating or dropping food
⚠️ Excessive drooling
⚠️ Red, swollen or bleeding gums
⚠️ Pawing at the mouth
⚠️ Yellow or brown teeth

If you notice any of these signs, bring your pet in immediately — dental disease progresses quickly.

🏠 Home Dental Care Tips

🪥

Brush Their Teeth

Use a pet-safe toothbrush and toothpaste (never human toothpaste). Even 2–3 times a week makes a big difference.

🦴

Dental Chews

Specially designed chews help reduce plaque and tartar while keeping your pet entertained.

📅

Regular Checkups

Annual professional dental cleanings prevent buildup that home brushing can't fully address.

🎯 Why Dental Health Matters Beyond the Mouth

❤️

Heart disease risk increases with untreated dental infections

🫁

Bacteria can travel to lungs causing respiratory issues

🫀

Liver and kidney damage from chronic oral bacteria

🧠

Chronic pain affects behaviour, mood and quality of life
